## Sleep Crisis Mechanics

### The 15mg Methylfolate Experiment

Your acute sleep disruption from brief high-dose methylfolate (15mg) was **nervous system recalibration, not true withdrawal**. This is important context:
- Panic at bedtime, heart pounding resolved within 1-2 weeks with proper support
- Not a sign the supplement was "wrong" but that dosing needed optimization
- Your nervous system was recalibrating to enhanced methylation activity

### Root Cause: Magnesium Depletion Pattern

**Magnesium depletion throughout the day triggers fight-or-flight at bedtime**

The critical discovery: if all magnesium taken at night or magnesium skipped during day:
- Afternoon/evening magnesium depletion causes anxiety
- Evening panic and heart pounding ensue
- This was the actual cause of sleep crisis, not methylfolate itself

**Solution**: Distributed magnesium across 5 doses (200mg morning, 200mg midday, 200mg afternoon, 200mg evening, 400mg bedtime)
- Prevents depletion that triggers panic
- Allows methylation to proceed safely throughout day
- Prevents evening fight-or-flight response

### Glycine: The Missing Piece for Sleep Architecture

**Glycine's temperature regulation effect is your missing sleep component**

Your sleep improves dramatically with glycine because:
- Temperature regulation enables sleep onset (body temperature must drop)
- Orexin inhibition (promotes wakefulness inhibition)
- GABA modulation (calming neurotransmitter support)
- Glycine + proper PS timing = stable sleep

**Critical timing**: 60-90 minutes before bed for optimal temperature drop

### Phosphatidylserine Timing: Concentrated vs. Spread

**PS timing is MORE critical than dose**

Discovery from v1.5:
- Concentrated 300mg dose at 6-7 PM (30-60 min to work)
- Takes effect by 7:30-8:30 PM
- Prevents cortisol spike that would otherwise disrupt sleep

**Why NOT bedtime dosing**: Cortisol spike happens earlier; by bedtime it's too late

**Why NOT spread throughout day**: Fragmented doses don't create sufficient concentrated effect on cortisol response

## Critical Protocol Mechanics

### Creatine Spares ~40% of SAM

**Creatine monohydrate: 3g daily spares approximately 40% of SAM (S-Adenosylmethionine)**

Practical effect:
- Your 2000mcg methylfolate + 3g creatine = ~3500mcg effective methylfolate dose
- Allows lower methylfolate supplementation while maintaining effect
- Reduces methylfolate-induced anxiety risk (lower absolute dose)
- Planned v2: 5g creatine = even more SAM sparing

This is why you don't need 5000mcg methylfolate despite compound heterozygous MTHFR.

### Distributed Magnesium Prevents Evening Depletion

**Why 5 doses instead of 1-2 large doses?**

Your body uses magnesium continuously for methylation all day. Single large evening doses create:
- Daytime depletion (causes anxiety, tension)
- Evening excess (doesn't solve daytime problem)
- Evening panic from magnesium "rush" effect

**Distributed approach (200mg each: morning, midday, afternoon, evening + 400mg bedtime)**:
- Prevents daytime depletion that triggers anxiety
- Maintains steady state for methylation cofactor needs
- Higher evening dose supports sleep without overshoot

## Genetic Profile Interaction Effects

**Enhanced Phase I with protected Phase II**

You have:

Result: You're actually in better position than CYP1B1 +/+ with GSTP1 variants would be

Your detox bottleneck is NAT2, not glutathione

**CYP1B1 +/+ (high Phase I) + NAT2 +/+ (slow Phase II acetylation) = double hit on carcinogen processing**

Carcinogens that need acetylation for clearance accumulate with you:
- Aromatic amines (tobacco, grilled meats)
- Heterocyclic amines (HCAs from high-temp cooking)
- Various other compounds that NAT2 typically processes

**Critical implication**: Your need to avoid smoking/charred meats is HIGHER than average

Your glutathione pathway (normal GSTP1) becomes your primary detox route for these substances

**Your heterozygous COMT status gives flexibility most genotypes don't have**

COMT variants create spectrum:

**Your advantage**:
- Not locked into extremes
- Moderate stress response capacity
- Flexibility to tolerate higher neurotransmitter doses better than pure worrier types
- Less pain sensitivity than pure warrior types

### Compound Heterozygous MTHFR: ~50-60% Enzyme Activity

**Important: Your compound heterozygosity is equivalent to homozygous C677T in activity loss**

Research shows:
- Homozygous C677T (+/+): ~70% activity loss

**Why this matters**: You need more methylation support than single-variant carriers, similar to homozygous C677T individuals

## Critical Implementation Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### Methylfolate Timing: Never After 2-3 PM

**Hard rule: Methylfolate by 10 AM maximum**

Why:
- Methylfolate is stimulating
- After 2 PM, causes sleep disruption
- Even 3 PM is cutting it close
- Better to be conservative (10 AM cutoff)

Your v1.5 protocol takes it with breakfast (8:00 AM) = ideal

### Timeline of Effects: Expect This Pattern

**Not immediate, but predictable**

Days 1-3:
- Initial improvements (panic reduced, sleep starts improving)
- Nervous system beginning recalibration
- Some residual anxiety possible

Days 4-7:
- Significant improvements (panic mostly resolved, sleep better)
- Better daily energy
- Mood baseline improving
- Heart pounding gone

Week 2:
- Sleep normalized
- Can assess if over/under-methylated
- Ready for dose adjustments if needed
- Baseline stability achieved

This is typical timeline. Faster if dosing/timing perfect, slower if off-protocol.

### Emergency Protocol: Box Breathing

When panic hits at bedtime:

1. **Sit up, don't force sleep**
2. **Box breathing**: 4 count in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold (repeat 5 min)
3. **Take additional 1000mg taurine** (you have this)
4. **Take 500-1000mg GABA sublingual** (emergency only)
5. **Wait 10-15 minutes**
6. **Try lying down again**
7. **Focus on long exhales** (4 in, 6-8 out)

If still can't sleep after 30 min:
- Get up (don't create negative sleep association)
- Do calm activity
- Try again after 20-30 min

### Simplification After Stabilization (3-4 weeks)

**Once sleep is stable, can reduce to maintenance**

From complex protocol down to:
- Morning: 2 multivitamin caps + 3g creatine + 200mg magnesium

- Evening: 200-400mg magnesium + 10g glycine powder + 500mcg melatonin
- Optional/as-needed: NAC (600mg), PS (100-200mg), L-theanine, taurine, GABA

Result: ~5-6 pills daily + one powder drink = much simpler

## When to Reassess

Re-evaluate protocol if:
- Sleep remains poor after week 2 with perfect adherence
- Anxiety increases beyond day 7
- New symptoms emerge (check against over-methylation signs)
- Energy remains low despite protocol (check undertherapy signs)
- Physical symptoms (pain, tension) aren't improving

Extend stabilization timeline if:
- Sleep cycle still normalizing at week 2 (3 weeks total is acceptable)
- Mood/anxiety taking longer to stabilize (person-dependent)
- Life stress interferes with protocol adherence (reschedule optimization)
